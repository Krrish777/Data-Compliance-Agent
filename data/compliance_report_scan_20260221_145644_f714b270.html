I
FMT-003 High severity
3,711Violations filed
Status · Fail

Transactions using Cash as the Payment Format with an Amount Paid exceeding 10,000 shall be flagged.

Explanation

These records violate the rule because they involve cash transactions whose Amount Paid exceeds the 10,000 threshold. The sample rows show amounts above the limit, yet the Amount Paid column values are not being surfaced to the flagging pipeline — pointing to a data-routing or upstream omission.

Policy · Clause

Section 3.2 — Large Transaction Reporting.
Applies when payment_format = 'Cash' AND amount_paid > 10,000.

Risk

Failure to flag and report large cash transactions risks non-compliance with BSA/AML reporting obligations, exposing the institution to regulatory fines and reputational damage.

Remediation · ordered steps
  1. Verify the Amount Paid column values for the 3,711 flagged records.
  2. Confirm payment_format = 'Cash' on each transaction.
  3. File the findings into the SAR/CTR queue and notify the compliance officer on duty.
II
AMT-001 High severity
3,711Violations filed
Status · Fail

Any transaction where Amount Paid exceeds 10,000 must be flagged for SAR/CTR review.

Explanation

The Amount Paid column carries values above the 10,000 threshold in 3,711 rows, yet the SAR/CTR flagging mechanism did not activate. This is a structural gap in the reporting pipeline rather than an individual data error — the rule is not wired into the system at all.

Policy · Clause

Section 3.2 — Large Transaction Reporting.
Applies globally whenever amount_paid > 10,000, regardless of payment format.

Risk

Missing SAR/CTR flags on large transactions is the single most material AML gap a regulator can cite. Penalties under the BSA can scale into seven figures per incident.

Remediation · ordered steps
  1. Confirm the Amount Paid column is correctly populated in upstream ingestion.
  2. Install a flagging hook for rows where amount_paid > 10,000.
  3. Re-run the scan and verify flags now propagate to the CTR queue.
III
AMT-002 High severity
3,711Violations filed
Status · Fail

Any transaction where Amount Received exceeds 10,000 must be flagged for SAR/CTR review.

Explanation

Amount Received exceeds the 10,000 threshold on 3,711 transactions spanning various From/To bank codes. None were flagged for SAR/CTR review. The volume mirrors AMT-001 almost exactly, suggesting the same upstream pipeline defect affects both sides of the transaction.

Policy · Clause

Section 3.2 — Large Transaction Reporting.
Symmetric counterpart to AMT-001; governs the receiving leg of the transaction.

Risk

Receiving-side reporting gaps are treated with the same severity as payment-side gaps. Both legs of a CTR-qualifying transfer must be reported independently.

Remediation · ordered steps
  1. Verify Amount Received values on the 3,711 violating rows.
  2. Ensure the SAR/CTR flag fires symmetrically on both paid and received legs.
  3. Trace the shared root cause with AMT-001 and remediate once.
IV
AMT-004 High severity
554Violations filed
Status · Fail

All transactions where Amount Paid exceeds one million in any currency shall trigger enhanced monitoring.

Explanation

554 transactions breach the 1,000,000 threshold but were not subjected to the enhanced-due-diligence pipeline. These are the highest-risk entries in the dataset; any one of them, unmonitored, represents a material AML exposure.

Policy · Clause

Section 3.2 — Enhanced Due Diligence for High-Value Transfers.
Independent of payment format.

Risk

Unmonitored seven-figure transactions are the textbook profile for layering in money laundering. Regulatory scrutiny is disproportionately severe on this tier.

Remediation · ordered steps
  1. Verify Amount Paid values on the 554 violating rows cross the 1M threshold.
  2. Enroll affected counterparties in the enhanced-monitoring program.
  3. Audit the EDD pipeline to confirm the threshold trigger is functional.
V
FMT-002 High severity
6Violations filed
Status · Fail

Transactions using Bitcoin as the Payment Format shall be automatically flagged for enhanced due diligence.

Explanation

Six transactions use Bitcoin as their payment_format yet bear no trace of EDD flagging. Low in absolute volume, the class is disproportionately high in risk weight — crypto rails are a standing subject of supervisory attention.

Policy · Clause

Section 3.2 — Crypto & Alternative Payment Rails.
Applies whenever payment_format = 'Bitcoin'.

Risk

Unflagged crypto transactions fail both AML and virtual-asset-service-provider expectations. The reputational downside dwarfs the operational cost of the flag.

Remediation · ordered steps
  1. Install automatic flagging on payment_format = 'Bitcoin'.
  2. Back-fill flags on the six historical rows and any prior-period equivalents.
  3. Apply EDD procedures to all flagged counterparties.

AMT-003 Medium severity
81Violations filed
Status · Fail

Micro-transactions: any transaction where Amount Paid is less than $1.00 must be flagged as a potential account-probing event.

Explanation

81 transactions carry an Amount Paid below $1.00 — the canonical signature of card-testing or account-probing activity. They form a smaller volume than the headline violations but warrant review because they can precede full fraud events by days.

Policy · Clause

Micro-transactions Rule.
Applies when amount_paid < 1.00 in any currency.

Risk

Unresolved micro-transactions frequently represent precursors to larger-value fraud or illicit-activity patterns — the cost of missing them is future incidents, not present ones.

Remediation · ordered steps
  1. Review the 81 transactions with Amount Paid below $1.00.
  2. Verify the legitimacy of each and mark false positives.
  3. Place involved accounts under heightened monitoring.
